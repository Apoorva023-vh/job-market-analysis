# 📊 Job Market Analysis — Data Science Industry (2025)

## Overview

This project presents a comprehensive analysis of the **Data Science job market in 2024**, derived from a dataset of real job postings across the United States. The analysis was built using **Power BI** and covers hiring trends, salary benchmarks, skill requirements, and industry demand.

## 🗂️ Report Sections

### 1. States with Most Job Postings
- **California** leads with ~210 postings — nearly 2× the next state.
- **Massachusetts** (130) and **New York** (115) follow closely.
- CA + MA + NY together account for **over 60%** of all postings.

### 2. Salary by State
- **DC** offers the highest max salary (~$140K); **CA** averages ~$115K.
- Southern states like KY and NC show narrower salary ranges.
- Location premium: DC/CA roles pay ~30% more than southern states.

### 3. Top Industries Hiring Data Scientists
| Rank | Industry | Job Count |
|------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | Biotech & Pharma | 95 |
| 2 | Insurance | 72 |
| 3 | Computer Hardware | 65 |
| 4 | IT Services | 60 |
| 5 | Health Care | 50 |

### 4. Top Companies by Job Openings
- **MassMutual** (10 openings) — insurance sector's growing DS investment.
- Pharma giants: **Takeda**, **AstraZeneca** building data teams aggressively.
- Defense/Research: **PNNL**, **MITRE** — good targets for senior talent.

### 5. Most In-Demand Job Titles
- **Data Scientist** (95 postings) — most sought-after title (~13% of all postings).
- Senior/Director-level roles outnumber entry-level — indicating a **mid-senior talent gap**.

### 6. Salary by Job Title
- **Director II** roles: $155K+ average — 50%+ premium over individual contributors.
- **Principal MLE** accumulates the most total compensation budget.

### 7. Skills Required
| Skill | Importance |
|-------|------------|
| Python | Universal — required across all roles |
| SQL | Second only to Python across all titles |
| AWS | Top cloud skill, especially for ML Engineers |
| Excel | Critical for analyst roles |

### 8. Education vs Salary
| Education | Avg Salary |
|-----------|------------|
| No Degree | $85K |
| Master's | $105K |
| PhD | $130K |

> Education is the **single strongest predictor** of salary in this dataset. PhD holders earn ~53% more than those without a degree.

## 💡 Key Insights

- **Python + SQL** = minimum viable skill set for any DS role.
- **Biotech & Pharma + Healthcare** = dominant DS employers (50%+ of top-5 postings).
- **Insurance sector** (MassMutual, Liberty Mutual) is an underrated DS employer.
- **AWS proficiency** adds a measurable salary premium.
- **Master's degree** unlocks most senior individual-contributor roles.

---

## 🎯 Strategic Recommendations

1. **Geographic Targeting** — Focus job postings in CA, MA, NY; use NC & VA as cost-efficient secondary markets.
2. **Mandatory Skills** — Require Python + SQL for all DS roles; AWS for senior/lead positions.
3. **Salary Bands** — $80–95K (Bachelor's) · $100–120K (Master's) · $125–155K (PhD).
4. **Industry Partnerships** — Partner with Biotech and Healthcare for talent pipelines.
5. **Senior Talent** — Offer equity, flexibility, and title clarity to attract Director/Principal-level candidates.
6. **Upskilling** — Invest in Python, SQL, and AWS training; internal promotions reduce hiring costs by 35–50%.
7. **Benchmarking** — Monitor MassMutual, PNNL, and AstraZeneca quarterly for JD language and salary trends.
